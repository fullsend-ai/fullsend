## The Problem

Red Hat needs rigorous data to make strategic decisions about AI agent adoption. Traditional manual experiment tracking:
- Takes 5-7 hours per experiment
- Often biased (people validate own ideas)
- Multi-week experiments abandoned (too painful)

**Result:** Strategic decisions based on gut feel, not evidence.

---

## The Solution

**Experiment Agent v2.5-Lite:** AI-powered experiment tracker that:
- **Reduces tracking time by 75-80%** (30-50 min vs. 5-7 hours)
- **Eliminates confirmation bias** (devil's advocate mode, strict metrics)
- **Enables multi-week experiments** (persistent memory across sessions)

## Key Features (v2.5-Lite)

**Core Capabilities:**
- ✅ Experiment design via conversation (12-18 min setup)
- ✅ Bias-corrected tracking (strict metrics, devil's advocate mode)
- ✅ Balanced reports (benefits + costs + contradictions)
- ✅ Progressive disclosure UX (1-3 questions at a time, no overwhelm)

**NEW in V4:**
- ✅ **Persistent memory** (multi-week experiments seamless)
- ✅ **Auto-load context** (zero re-explaining when you return)
- ✅ **Mid-experiment docs** (add context anytime)
- ✅ **Archive experiments** (build experiment library)

**Also Includes:**
- ✅ Image/screenshot support (essential for design experiments)
- ✅ Multi-person collaboration (teams can contribute observations)
- ✅ Experiment templates (clone similar experiments)
- ✅ Editable observations (fix mistakes anytime)
